Banganga Tank

Banganga Tank is an ancient water tank located in the Walkeshwar Temple Complex in Malabar Hill. This sacred site is a remarkable reminder of Mumbai's ancient history, dating back to the 12th century.

History

According to legend, Lord Rama stopped here during his journey to Lanka. When he needed water, he shot an arrow into the ground, creating a tributary of the Ganges, hence the name Banganga (Baan means arrow, Ganga refers to the River Ganges).

Architecture

The tank is surrounded by steps (ghats) made of stone, with numerous temples and shrines lining its perimeter. The rectangular tank measures 120 meters by 40 meters and is an excellent example of ancient Indian architecture and engineering. The surrounding area features traditional Indian architectural elements, including carved stone temples and dharamshalas (rest houses).
