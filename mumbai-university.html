University of Mumbai

The University of Mumbai, established in 1857, is one of the oldest and most prestigious educational institutions in India. Its iconic Victorian Gothic architecture makes it a significant heritage site in Mumbai.

History

The university was established in 1857 during the British rule and was one of the first three universities in India. It was modeled after similar universities in the United Kingdom, particularly the University of London.

Architecture

The university's main building, Convocation Hall, and Rajabai Clock Tower are prime examples of Victorian Gothic architecture. The Rajabai Tower, standing at 280 feet, was designed by Sir George Gilbert Scott and completed in 1878. Its design combines Venetian and Gothic styles with local architectural elements.
