Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch monument built during the 20th century in Bombay, India. The monument was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder when they visited India in 1911.

History

The foundation stone was laid on 31 March 1911, and the structure was completed in 1924. The Gateway was later used as a symbolic ceremonial entrance to India for Viceroys and the new Governors of Bombay.

Architecture

The structure is an arch made of basalt, 26 meters (85 feet) high. The architectural style combines elements of the Roman triumphal arch and traditional Hindu and Muslim architectural styles.
